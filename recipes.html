Recipe 1: Brownies

Ingredients:

  • 2 sticks (1 cup) of unsalted butter
  • 2 cups of semi-sweet chocolate chips
  • 1 and 1/2 cups of granulated sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • 1 cup of all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt

Peanut Butter Topping:

  • 1 cup of creamy peanut butter
  • 1/2 cup of powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup of softened unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • Just a pinch of salt

Directions:

  1. Start by preheating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9x13-inch baking pan and line it with parchment paper, leaving some paper hanging over the sides for easy removal later.
  2. In a microwave-safe bowl or using a double boiler, melt 2 cups of semi-sweet chocolate chips and 2 sticks of butter together until they’re smooth. Let it cool for a bit.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together 1 and 1/2 cups of granulated sugar, 4 large eggs, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract until it’s all mixed up nicely.
  4. Slowly pour that smooth chocolate mixture into the egg mixture, stirring until everything is well combined.
  5. In another bowl, sift together 1 cup of all-purpose flour, 1/4 cup of unsweetened cocoa powder, and 1/2 teaspoon of salt. Gradually add these dry ingredients to your wet mixture, stirring until they’re just combined. Don’t overdo it!
  6. Pour this delightful brownie batter into your prepared baking pan, spreading it out evenly.
  7. Now, in a separate bowl, mix together 1 cup of creamy peanut butter, 1/2 cup of powdered sugar, 1/4 cup of softened butter, 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract, and just a pinch of salt until it’s super smooth.
  8. Drop spoonfuls of this peanut butter goodness onto your brownie batter.
  9. Take a knife or a toothpick and swirl that peanut butter into the brownie batter to make beautiful swirls.
  10. Pop the pan into your preheated oven and bake for around 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with just a few moist crumbs (not wet batter). Keep an eye on it as baking times can vary.
  11. Let those amazing brownies cool in the pan for about 20 minutes, then use the parchment paper to lift them out onto a cooling rack.
  12. Once they’re completely cool, cut them into squares and indulge in your homemade, rich semi-sweet chocolate brownies with that heavenly creamy peanut butter swirl on top!
